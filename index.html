Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar

The man who drafted the Indian constitution.

Here's a time line of Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar's life:

  • 1891 - Born in Mhow, Central Provinces
  • 1906 - Converted to Buddhism
  • 1913 - Completed his degree in economics and political science from Bombay University
  • 1915 - Completed his master's degree in economics from Bombay University
  • 1923 - Earned a degree in law from Bombay University
  • 1924 - Appointed as the Professor of Political Economy at the Sydenham
  • 1927 - Elected to the Bombay Legislative Council
  • 1929 - Published his book "Annihilation of Caste"
  • 1932 - Elected as the chairman of the Bombay Presidency Committee of the Round Table Conference in London
  • 1936 - Appointed as the principal of the Government Law College, Bombay
  • 1937 - Elected to the Central Legislative Assembly
  • 1942 - Launched the Independent Labour Party
  • 1946 - Appointed as the chairman of the Constitution Drafting Committee
  • 1950 - The Constitution of India was adopted on November 26, 1950
  • 1956 - Died on December 6, 1956, in Delhi.

"Dr. Ambedkar's vision of a modern, democratic and inclusive India is more relevant today than ever before." - Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi
